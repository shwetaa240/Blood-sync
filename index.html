Blood Types Needed

The demand for blood donations fluctuates due to various factors such as weather changes, holiday seasons, and unforeseen emergencies. Each day, patients in critical need rely on the generosity of donors like you. With less than 10% of the population actively donating, your contribution is vital to ensuring a steady supply for those in need. Regular donors play an indispensable role in maintaining our blood inventories, particularly during times of crisis. By scheduling an appointment today, you can make a profound impact and help save lives. Join us in this lifesaving mission—your donation could be the difference between life and death for someone in need.

Making Your First Donation

Taking the step to donate blood for the first time is an admirable decision that can save lives. Whether you’re motivated by a personal experience or simply want to help those in need, understanding the donation process can make your first experience smooth and enjoyable. Here’s a straightforward guide to walk you through each step of making your first blood donation-

Step 1:

Complete a health check questionnaire and drink 500ml of water.

Step 2:

Have a private health screening with a friendly member of the team.

Step 3:

Take a seat and then donate blood happily for 5-10 minutes.

Step 4:

Enjoy a free drink and snack while you rest for 15 minutes.

Step 5:

You're all done. Your donation will help save up to three lives.
